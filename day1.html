Just a quick background of myself.

I studied Diploma in Computer Engineering in Polytechnic. I only began to realise it was more of hardware
than software. I realised it wasn't something that's for me. Learning about computers and how things work was cool and stuff but
deep inside I just knew it wasn't for me. Then, there was one semester where I took a basic programming class using C++ and I loved it.

After graduating, I began my National Service ; a mandatory, 2 year full-time service in the Singapore Police Force.
4 months since my enlisting into the Force, I was interested in being a Software Engineering as a career after my service. So I picked up
a Python course on Udemy. I only managed to complete the pure basics section of the course and that's where I just suddenly lost interest.
THe reason behind that was probably because I didn't know specifically what kind of Software Engineering suits me. Or maybe Python just seemed
too daunting for a noob like me.

So, months later, I came across a video on my reccomended page on YouTube.
After watching the video, I researched a little on other people's portfolios and found them to be very cool. I didn't want to expect much
so I dove straight into the world of Web Development. Making aesthetically pleasing and intuitive websites makes me feel excited to learn more about
Web Development.

Now, here I am, officially starting my journey as a self-taught Web Developer, Currently in a course called The Complete 2020 Web Development Bootcamp by Angela Yu and so far, it's going great!

There are the things I learned so far:

  • How websites work: in short; HTML is the structure, CSS is the aesthetics, JavaScript is the behaviour.
  • Basic HTML Syntax
  • HTML boilerplate/basic structure
  • How create a Project in Atom
  • The anatomy of an HTML Tag
